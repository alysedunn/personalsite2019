Novice Newcommer to Professional Programmer

9Feb- 2019


One might call me a 'self taught' software engineer. By that, I mean I don't have a degree in computer science or anything close to it, and I learned how to code without any 'formal' education (although I have completed a few coding courses and have received lots of amazing support from my community as I learned how to code, hence the quotes when I say that I'm 'self taught'.

As a 'self-taught' engineer who now works for a rather prominent, NYC-based tech company —Venmo —I frequently get asked: 'How did you do it? How did you transition from a completely non-technical role, into a full-time software engineering role?'

Whenever I get asked this question, I suspect that the real question that people have isn't necessarily how I personally made this transition, but more generally, how does the average, non-technical person make such a drastic career transition into development? So, in an attempt to answer that deeper question, I posed it to five of my self-taught colleagues, so that we can all gain a bit more insight into how some have successfully made the leap.
